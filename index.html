About KindNest

KindNest recognizes that international students face significant and unique practical challenges when settling into life in Edinburgh. With over 24,000 international students at the University of Edinburgh alone, and tuition fees ranging from ¬£15,250 to ¬£43,300 annually, the financial pressure to find supplemental income is immense.

Our Mission

To create a supportive digital ecosystem that addresses the core challenges faced by international students in Edinburgh, helping them thrive academically, professionally, and personally while contributing to Scotland's reputation as a top study destination.

Key Challenges We Address

Data from NUS Scotland, Wonkhe, and other research reveals a crisis across housing, employment, finance, and mental health. These interconnected challenges create significant barriers for international students.

Employment Barriers

Complex visa restrictions and employer hesitancy create "soul-crushing" job searches

Housing Crisis

21% of international students have experienced homelessness during studies

Financial Pressure

10% have used foodbanks, 29% considered leaving due to money worries

Mental Health

50% of international students experience poor mental health while studying

The Housing Crisis: Scarcity & Homelessness

Edinburgh faces a severe shortage of over 13,000 student beds, making international students particularly vulnerable to homelessness.

Student Distress: A Compounding Effect

Financial and social instability creates a cascade of mental health challenges that directly impact academic success.

The KindNest Solution: A Three-Pillar Platform

Our platform directly tackles the identified challenges by creating a centralized, student-focused ecosystem for essential needs: finding work, securing housing, and managing resources efficiently.
